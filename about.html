Where Origin Meets Tradition

Ethiopian Ball Clippers was born from a simple observation: the world's best coffee origins and the world's most refined brewing method had never been properly united.

East African coffees—with their extraordinary complexity, vibrant acidity, and floral depth—are uniquely suited to the ultra-fine grind and slow extraction of Turkish coffee. We make that connection direct, transparent, and transformative.

Every lot we source is selected specifically for how it will perform in a cezve. We work directly with cooperatives across six East African nations, ensuring fair prices and exceptional quality at every step.

Our Partners

Across Six Nations

Direct relationships with cooperatives and washing stations across East Africa.

🇪🇹

Ethiopia

12 Cooperatives

Yirgacheffe, Sidamo, Guji, and Harrar regions. Heirloom varietals at 1,700–2,200m altitude.

Our longest-standing partnerships, including the Konga Cooperative in Yirgacheffe and the Hambela Washing Station in Guji. These relationships deliver some of the most complex and floral coffees in our portfolio.

🇰🇪

Kenya

6 Cooperatives

Nyeri, Kirinyaga, and Embu counties. SL28 and SL34 varietals with bold character.

Kenyan coffees bring unmatched intensity to the cezve. Our partners in Nyeri County produce AA and AB grades with the wine-like depth and blackcurrant notes that define Kenyan specialty.

🇺🇬

Uganda

4 Cooperatives

Mount Elgon and Rwenzori ranges. Emerging specialty Arabica with rich body.

Uganda's specialty Arabica sector is experiencing a renaissance. Our Mount Elgon partners produce SL14 and SL28 varietals with chocolate, stone fruit, and a full body perfect for Turkish brewing.

🇷🇼

Rwanda

5 Cooperatives

Huye, Nyamasheke, and Gakenke districts. Red Bourbon with silky sweetness.

Rwanda's Red Bourbon coffees are a revelation in the cezve—silky body, caramel sweetness, and stone fruit complexity. Our partners operate fully washed processing with meticulous cherry selection.

🇧🇮

Burundi

3 Cooperatives

Kayanza and Ngozi provinces. High-altitude lots with fruit-forward complexity.

Burundi's Kayanza province sits at elevations above 1,800m, producing coffees with bright acidity, complex fruit character, and a sweetness that intensifies through Turkish brewing's slow extraction.

🇹🇿

Tanzania

4 Cooperatives

Kilimanjaro, Arusha, and Mbeya regions. Peaberry and AA selections.

Tanzanian Peaberry is legendary in the cezve. The dense, round beans extract evenly in the ultra-fine grind, producing cups with sparkling acidity, cocoa undertones, and remarkable clarity.
