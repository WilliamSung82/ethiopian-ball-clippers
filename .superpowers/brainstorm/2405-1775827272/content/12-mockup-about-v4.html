Our Story

Bridging Two Coffee Cultures

From the highland farms of East Africa to the cezves of Turkish coffee houses — we connect the world's oldest coffee origins with its most revered brewing tradition.

Discover

The Route

Real geography. Real partnerships. Hover pins to explore each origin.

🇪🇹 Ethiopia

The birthplace of coffee. Heirloom varietals from 1,700–2,200m. Floral, fruity, extraordinarily complex.

12 cooperatives · 4,200+ farmers · Yirgacheffe, Sidamo, Harrar, Guji, Limu

🇰🇪 Kenya

Bold, wine-like AA grades. SL28/SL34 varietals with intense blackcurrant and grapefruit character.

5 estates · Nyeri, Kirinyaga, Embu, Murang'a

🇺🇬 Uganda

Mountain Arabica with full body. Nutty, chocolatey depth perfect for Turkish brewing.

2 cooperatives · Mount Elgon, Rwenzori

🇷🇼 Rwanda

Silky Red Bourbons known for caramel sweetness and stone fruit. Clean, luxurious body.

4 cooperatives · 1,700m+ · Huye, Nyamasheke, Gakenke

🇧🇮 Burundi

Hidden gem. Clean, sweet cups with red apple and vanilla notes from highland smallholders.

2 cooperatives · Kayanza, Ngozi

🇹🇿 Tanzania

Volcanic Peaberry from Kilimanjaro slopes. Bright acidity with dark chocolate and black tea.

3 estates · Kilimanjaro, Arusha, Mbeya

🇹🇷 Turkey — Destination

Where African origins meet the art of cezve brewing. Serving independent Turkish coffee shops.

Your cezve awaits ☕
